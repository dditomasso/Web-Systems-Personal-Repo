Article I Annotation

Section 1

This Section delcares that the Senate and the House of Representratives are members parts of Congress.

The United States Goverment ensures that power is distributed equally amongst the goverment, states, and citizens. Having both the House of Represenatatives and the Senate guarntee this equal distribution of power amongst the three groups, such that one group does not have an avantage over the other (checks and balances).

Section 2

This section declares that members of the House of Representatives will be elected into office by individuals from every state. To run for the House of Representatives, you must be 25 years or older, and must be a Citizen of the United States for at least 7 years. Additionally, Each state gets at least one representative reguardless of population number.

This seciton ensures that each of the states recieve equal representation within the House of Representatives. If the population of a state is low, then under this article they are guarnteed at least one representative, and contraversliy, if the population of the state is high, then they are capped a certian number of representatives, to ensure that the larger states do not hold an excess amount of power over the smaller states.

Section 3

This section declares that the Senate will be composed of two Senators from each State, reguardless of population. Elections occur every two years, and Senators must be at least 30 years old, and be a US Citizen for at least 9 years, and cannot live in the state they are representing once elected. Additionally, The Vice President of the United States will be President of the Senate, but will have no vote on matters, unless the vote is equally divided. The Senate also holds the power to try the President of the United States for Impeachment.

Esssentially, the Senate allows states a fixed number of senators (2), reguardless of population. This means, this gives smaller states more of an opportuinity to voice their votes equally, whereas in the House of Representatives, larger states may have more of a say due to their large population size.

Section 4

This section declares that how elections are held for the House of Represntatives and Senators are ulitmatley up to the states, however Congress holds the right to change their process at any time with valid reason.

This process ensures that elections process are being impartial and effective, but also gives the States the opportuinity to excerise their freedom of choice.

Section 5

This section declares that each House is in charge of determining its own rules and proceedings. Additionally, a log must be kept of all proceedings, and all votes should be recorded within this log.

This article ensures that the Houses have the right to exercize their autonomy by being in chare of their own rules, but also must be held accountable for their actions, hence the purpose of the log.

Section 6

This section delcares that no Senator or Representative will, during the time they are elected, be appointed to any civil office under the authority of the United States.

This is extremely important, becuase this ensures that the representative chosen puts all of their focus and attention onto the job they were elected for, which allows the citizens voices to be heard wihtin the Goverment.

Section 7

This seciton states that in order for a bill to pass, it has to pass through the House of Representatives, the Senate, and have the approval of the President. If any of these three object (vote nay), then the bill will not come to pass.

This article greatly uses the checks and balances system, in which all Houses must agree on a bill, which ensures the legitimacy of the bill and it has the best intrest of the United States and its citizens in mind.

Section 8

This section declares that Congress has the power To lay and collect Taxes, Duties, Imposts and Excises, to pay the Debts and provide for the common Defence and general Welfare of the United States. Additonally, this article states that Congress can raise and support armies, to maintain a Navy, and create rules and regukations for the military forces.

If the President of the United States had the sole power to declare war, raise an army, and be in control of the Navy, too much power would fall into the hands of a single individual. Therefore it is crucial that Congress also have a say in crucial matters such this, to ensure that no rash decisions are made.

Section 9

This section declares that no Tax or Duty shall be laid on Articles exported from any State. Additonally, no preference shall be given by any regulation of commerce or revenue to the ports of one state over those of another.

This article ensures that no state is favored over another, espically in the fields of commerce and port trading.

Section 10

This section declares that no State shall, without the consent of Congress, keep Troops, or Ships of War in time of Peace, enter into any Agreement or Compact with another State, or with a foreign Power, or engage in War, unless actually invaded, or in such imminent Danger as will not admit of delay.

This article's main goal is to maintain peace. By declaring that war ships and other military persona are illegal to own in any individual state in times of peace, it helps to prevent furture conflicts and poetential conflicts.
